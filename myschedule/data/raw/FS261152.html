Inhalt Is it possible to improve one's wellbeing? This seminar explores the interdisciplinary science of wellbeing and the interventions that promote it. We will examine and discuss key questions such as whether well-being represents more than the mere absence of psychopathology, how well-being can be enhanced through evidence-based approaches, and how such interventions can be integrated into both clinical practice and everyday life. Students will engage with research from clinical psychology, positive psychology, behavioral science, and neuroscience to critically assess theoretical models, research findings, and practical tools. Through readings, discussions, experiential exercises, and personal projects, students will deepen their understanding of how wellbeing can be conceptualized, measured, and cultivated in various contexts. Students may also experience an increase in their own wellbeing.
Lernziele By the end of the seminar, students will be able to:
• Understand how wellbeing and its interventions relate to and differ from psychopathology and articulate how psychotherapy links both constructs.
• Connect and compare scientific theories on wellbeing from multiple perspectives.
• Critically evaluate existing wellbeing interventions and understand how to design interventions to achieve greater wellbeing.
• Understand what contributes to wellbeing from various perspectives.

Additionally, students will learn skills relevant for the rest of their studies and bachelor thesis:
• Read, interpret, and critically evaluate scientific studies.
• Ask critical questions, express, and defend their viewpoint in discussions.
• Lead and facilitate discussions.
• Understand and practice designing and implementing interventions.
• Implement a small, evidence-based intervention in their own lives, track changes and reflect on them.
• Present, communicate, and discuss findings in a professional manner.
• Write in a scientific manner.
